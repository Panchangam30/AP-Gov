# Creator's Statement

**Project:** *Power, Divided* — an AP Government photo project
**Format:** A digital photo carousel of fifteen everyday objects, each one matched to a government term, with an analysis and a rating page.

## My Message

The whole project is built around one big idea, the Founders were scared of any one person or group having too much power, so they built the entire system to divide it up. That fear is the reason we have things like federalism, checks and balances, and separation of powers. I wanted to show that this idea isn't just something locked inside the Constitution, it's actually all around us in regular objects we don't even think about. The message I want people to walk away with is that power in America is supposed to be spread out, checked, shared, and connected to the people, and it's never supposed to sit in one place.

## Creative Choices

I chose everyday objects on purpose instead of using flags or pictures of the government. The idea behind this is that if I can take something boring like a water bottle case or a strainer and connect it to a real government term, then it proves the concept is simple enough to see anywhere. I also made it a carousel instead of a long list so people slow down and look at one object at a time, the same way you'd actually study one idea before moving to the next. To add on, I added a rating page so the viewer isn't just reading, they get to decide for themselves how well each object actually matches its term, which makes them think about it instead of just believing me.

## Symbolism

Each object is a symbol for how power gets handled. The water bottle case stands for federalism because all the bottles are held under one wrapper but stay separate, just like the states. The backpack with separate compartments is separation of powers since every pocket has its own job. The scale is the rule of law because it treats whatever you put on it the same way every time. The strainer is selective incorporation since it lets some things through and holds the rest back. Even the chess/strategy game is checks and balances, because every move can get countered so nobody just wins. Every single object points back to the same theme of power being divided instead of concentrated.

## What I Want Viewers to Notice

I want viewers to notice that all fifteen objects are really telling one story. At first they look random, a trophy, a magnet, a microphone, but once you read each one you start to see they all connect to the same idea about power being split up and limited. I also want people to notice how normal these objects are. The point is that the rules holding our government together aren't far away, they're the same patterns we see in stuff sitting around our houses. If someone finishes the project and starts seeing government concepts in their own everyday life, then I did what I set out to do.
